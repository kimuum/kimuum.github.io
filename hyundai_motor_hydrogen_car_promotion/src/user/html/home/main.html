"Starting in 1998, Hyundai officially began its journey with hydrogen. Hyundai's fuel cell system has evolved through several generations, with each one marking its own significance, and making improvements from the last. This progress at Hyundai is still ongoing and has never stopped.

Back in 2000, Hyundai introduced its first hydrogen-powered vehicle prototype, the Santa Fe FCEV, featuring the world premiere 350-bar high-pressure hydrogen storage tank. With its excellent performance without carbon emissions, together with reduced noise and vibration, it won the best FCV award at the Michelin Challenge Bibendum in 2001. The following year, it completed the Fuel Cell Road Rally in California alongside top carmakers, showcasing its advanced technologies in real-world driving scenarios.

Today, Hyundai's Fuel Cell system legacy continues with the HTWO brand."
Hyundai’s First Hydrogen-Powered Vehicle Prototype
